FINAL INSIGHTS & CONCLUSION
1. Income Distribution
Majority of individuals earn <=50K.
A smaller portion earns >50K, indicating income imbalance in the dataset.
2. Age & Working Hours Impact
Individuals earning >50K work more hours per week on average.
Higher income is generally associated with higher work commitment.
3. Education vs Income
Higher education levels (Bachelors, Masters, Professional school) contribute more to >50K income.
Lower education levels mostly fall under <=50K.
4. Gender vs Income
Males dominate the >50K income group.
Females are more represented in the <=50K category.
Indicates a noticeable gender income gap.
CONCLUSION
This analysis shows that income is strongly influenced by education level, working hours, and gender.
Higher education and longer working hours increase the likelihood of earning more than 50K, while income inequality is evident across gender lines.
